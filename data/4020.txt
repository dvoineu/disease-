# Percentage of People with Asthma who Smoke

Minus

Related Pages

**Nearly 17% of people without asthma smoke. But surprisingly, even more
people with asthma smoke. About 21% of people with asthma smoke, even though
cigarette smoke is known to trigger asthma attacks.**

Smoking rates are different from state to state. The percentage of people with
asthma who smoke ranges from about 12% in Minnesota and Utah to about 32% in
Kentucky. The states with the highest percentage of people with asthma who
smoke are clustered in the Midwest and South regions. See the map above for
details.

CDC's National Asthma Control Program (NACP) was created in 1999 to help the
millions of people with asthma in the United States gain control over their
disease. The NACP conducts national asthma surveillance and funds states to
help them improve their asthma surveillance and to focus efforts and resources
where they are needed.

**Source:** Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS), 2010

* Includes persons who answered "yes" to the questions: "Have you ever been told by a doctor, nurse, or other health professional that you had asthma?" and "Do you still have asthma?"

¶ U.S. Total includes 50 states plus DC and excludes Puerto Rico and the two
territories

** Estimate is unreliable if the unweighted sample size for the denominator
was < 50 or relative standard error was > 0.30
