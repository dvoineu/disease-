# Foodborne Outbreaks

Minus

Related Pages

During a multistate foodborne disease outbreak, CDC serves as lead coordinator
between public health partners to detect the outbreak, define its size and
extent, and to identify the source.

Public health officials investigate outbreaks to control them, so more people
do not get sick in the outbreak, and to learn how to prevent similar outbreaks
from happening in the future.
