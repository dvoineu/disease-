# Brochures, Fact Sheets, and Action Plans

Minus

Related Pages

![Doctor gives patient a dose from an
inhaler](/asthma/images/doctor_with_patient.png)

You can control your asthma and avoid an attack by taking your medicine
exactly as your doctor or other medical professional tells you to do and by
avoiding things that can cause an attack. With your doctor's help, make your
own asthma management plan so that you know what to do based on your own
symptoms. Here are helpful resources for you:

![You Can Control Your Asthma - Brochure
Cover](/asthma/images/brochure_control_cdc.jpg)

### You Can Control Your Asthma [PDF - 3.98 MB]

This brochure contains the basic facts about asthma--what it is, its effects,
and how it is diagnosed and treated.

### Asthma Care: Be Ready for Natural Disasters

Information about asthma care before, during, and after a hurricane, tropical
storm, or flooding.

### Asthma Action Plan

Work with your doctor or other medical professional to use this plan. Write
down how to manage your asthma both routinely and during an attack. Look on
the back for a list of possible asthma triggers and ways to avoid them.

### Asthma and Outdoor Air Pollution [PDF - 499 KB]external icon

This fact sheet provides information for people with asthma on understanding
and using outdoor air quality warnings.
