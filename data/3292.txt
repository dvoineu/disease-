# 1918 Pandemic Influenza: Three Waves

### The 1918 influenza pandemic occurred in three waves and was the most
severe pandemic in history.

![Graphic: The 1918 influenza pandemic occurred in three waves and was the
most severe pandemic in recent history.](/flu/pandemic-
resources/1918-commemoration/images/1918-three-waves-header.jpg)

* * *

###  **First Wave** - Spring 1918

![Graphic: first wave - spring 1918](/flu/pandemic-
resources/1918-commemoration/images/first-wave.jpg)

![Soldiers resting at Camp Funston in Fort Riley, Kansas.](/flu/pandemic-
resources/1918-commemoration/images/spring-1918-flu.jpg)

The first outbreak of flu-like illnesses was detected in the U.S. in March,
with more than 100 cases reported at Camp Funston in Fort Riley, Kansas.

![Graphic: globe showing worldwide spread of flu](/flu/pandemic-
resources/1918-commemoration/images/pandemic-spread.jpg)

During 1918, the U.S. was engaged in WWI. Hundreds and thousands of U.S.
soldiers traveled across the Atlantic to deploy for war. The mass troop
movement contributed to the global spread of flu.

**More people died during the 1918 pandemic than the total number of military
and civilian deaths that resulted from World War I.**

* * *

![Graphic: Second wave - fall 1918](/flu/pandemic-
resources/1918-commemoration/images/second-wave.jpg)

###  **Second Wave** - Fall 1918

![Caduceus symbol](/flu/pandemic-resources/1918-commemoration/images/caduceus-
flu.jpg)

In 1918, many health professionals served in the U. S. military during WWI,
resulting in shortages of medical personnel around the U.S. The economy
suffered as businesses and factories were forced to close due to sickness
amongst workers.

![Graph: 3 different waves of influenza](/flu/pandemic-
resources/1918-commemoration/images/death-chart.jpg)

There were 3 different waves of illness during the pandemic, starting in March
1918 and subsiding by summer of  1919\. The pandemic peaked in the U.S. during
the second wave, in the fall of 1918. This highly fatal second wave was
responsible for most of the U.S. deaths attributed to the pandemic.

* * *

![Graphic: Third wave - winter 1918](/flu/pandemic-
resources/1918-commemoration/images/third-wave.jpg)

###  **Third Wave** - Winter 1918

![The Motor Corps of St. Louis chapter of the American Red Cross on ambulance
duty during the influenza epidemic, October 1918.](/flu/pandemic-
resources/1918-commemoration/images/third-wave-flu-pandemic.jpg)

The Motor Corps of St. Louis chapter of the American Red Cross on ambulance
duty during the influenza epidemic, October 1918.

A third wave of illness occurred during the winter and spring of 1919, adding
to the pandemic death toll. The third wave of the pandemic subsided during the
summer of 1919.

**An estimated 1/3 of the world 's population was infected with the 1918 flu
virus - resulting in at least 50 million deaths worldwide.**
