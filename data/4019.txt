**World Asthma Day Is May 1, 2018**  
_May is Asthma Awareness Month_



Over 22 million people living in the United States have asthma, a chronic
lifelong disease that affects the lungs. Asthma can cause wheezing,
breathlessness, chest tightness, and coughing. Although asthma cannot be
cured, it is possible to manage asthma to reduce and prevent asthma attacks,
also called episodes. In the United States, approximately half of people with
asthma had at least one asthma attack in 2012. More children (55%) than adults
(49%) had an attack.

Asthma attacks cause adults to miss work and children to miss school. These
dangerous and sometimes life-threatening episodes reduce the quality of life
for people with asthma. The good news is that we can raise awareness about
asthma and how it can be controlled.

People with asthma can prevent asthma attacks if they learn how to avoid
asthma triggers like tobacco smoke, mold, outdoor air pollution, and colds and
flu. Asthma episodes can also be prevented by using inhaled corticosteroids
and other prescribed daily long-term control medicines correctly.
