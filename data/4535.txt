# Food Safety for Special Events, Seasons, and Holidays

Minus

Related Pages

Make food safety a part of your party, holiday or event plans.  Follow these
tips to stay healthy while cooking for parties and during special events and
holiday celebrations.

![Image of a Turkey in a oven](/foodsafety/images/comms/turkeytime_350px.jpg)

Food Safety Tips for Your Holiday Turkey

Follow these tips to help you safely prepare your next holiday turkey meal.

![Image of table will food](/foodsafety/images/comms/food-Safety-Tips-
small.jpg)

Food Safety Tips for the Holidays

Feasting with family and friends is part of many holiday celebrations. Follow
these simple tips to keep safe from food poisoning, or foodborne illness,
during the holidays.

![Close up of woman in the kitchen](/foodsafety/images/comms/Chitlins-
traditional-southern-food-400w.jpg)

Preparing Chitlins Safely

Chitlins are a traditional Southern food served during winter holidays. If
you're getting ready to cook chitlins, sometimes called chitterlings, it's
important to handle and cook these pork intestines the right way.

![Family in the kitchen making cookies](/foodsafety/images/comms/no-raw-
dough_400px.jpg)

Say No to Raw Dough!

Raw dough can contain bacteria that can make you sick. Follow these safety
tips when handling raw dough or batter.

![buffet of food](/foodsafety/images/buffet-party-food-safety.png)

Food Safety for Buffets and Parties

Use these tools and tips to help prevent food poisoning every time you prepare
food in the kitchen.

![game day wings](/foodsafety/images/game-day-wings.png)

Game Day Food Safety Tips

Tackling a buffet at your game day gathering? Practice these game rules and
keep the runs on the field.

![meat on a grill](/foodsafety/images/grilling-safely.jpg)

How to Grill Safely

Food poisoning peaks in the summer months when warmer temperatures cause
foodborne germs to flourish. Follow these steps for a safe and enjoyable
grilling season.
