Q fever is a disease caused by the bacteria _Coxiella burnetii_. This bacteria
naturally infects some animals, such as goats, sheep, and cattle. _C.
burnetii_ bacteria are found in the birth products (i.e. placenta, amniotic
fluid), urine, feces, and milk of infected animals. People can get infected by
breathing in dust that has been contaminated by infected animal feces, urine,
milk, and birth products. Some people never get sick; however, those who do
usually develop flu-like symptoms including fever, chills, fatigue, and muscle
pain.
