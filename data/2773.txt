# Micronutrient Malnutrition Prevention and Control

Minus

Related Pages

![mothers with their children](/nutrition/micronutrient-
malnutrition/images/immpact-banner-0522.jpg)

The International Micronutrient Malnutrition Prevention and Control (IMMPaCt)
Program works with global partners to contribute CDC skills and resources to
eliminate vitamin and mineral deficiencies (micronutrient malnutrition) among
vulnerable populations throughout the world. Established by the CDC in 2000,
IMMPaCt focuses primarily on helping eliminate deficiencies in iron, vitamin
A, iodine, folate, and zinc.

Deficiencies in micronutrients such as iron, iodine, vitamin A, folate and
zinc affect nearly one-third of the world's population, and the consequences
can be devastating.1 For more information, see Micronutrient Facts.

By helping countries develop and operate appropriate assessment, monitoring
and evaluation, systems, IMMPaCt and its global partners work to enable
national governments, food industries and civic organizations to successfully
implement interventions such as mass food fortification, supplementation and
home fortification in order to eliminate vitamin and mineral deficiencies. For
more information, see About the Program.
