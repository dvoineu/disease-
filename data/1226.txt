Basic Information

![Photo of a father and his adult
son](/cancer/prostate/images/2men-300x200.jpg)

Learn what prostate cancer is, who is at risk, and about symptoms, screening,
diagnosis, and treatment for prostate cancer.

Statistics

![Photo of a person looking at statistics about the United States on a digital
tablet](/cancer/prostate/images/usmap-tablet-300x200.jpg)

Prostate cancer is the second most common cancer among men in the United
States. The Data Visualizations Tool provides detailed statistics.

Health Tips

![Photo of a doctor explaining treatment to a senior man on digital
table](/cancer/prostate/images/doctor-patient-tablet-300x200.jpg)

Get an overview of prostate cancer screening and questions to ask your doctor
before you decide to get tested or treated for prostate cancer.

Should I Get Tested for Prostate Cancer?

It's important for men to talk about screening (testing) with their doctor.
This video helps men understand their prostate cancer screening options.

Blog Post: Real Talk about Prostate Health

![Photo of Demetrius
Parker](https://blogs.cdc.gov/cancer/files/2019/08/demetrius-150.jpg)

"It's tough to make a decision to screen for prostate cancer or to wait and
watch for symptoms," says CDC's Demetrius Parker in this blog post.

"That's why talking with our doctor is the key. Our physician can help us
avoid unnecessary tests and treatments and make the choice that fits our life,
our personal values, and individual circumstances."

CDC works with partners to reduce the burden of prostate cancer in the United
States. Learn what CDC is doing.
