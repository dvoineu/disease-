# Melioidosis

Minus

Related Pages

Melioidosis, also called Whitmore's disease, is an infectious disease that can
infect humans or animals. The disease is caused by the bacterium
_Burkholderia pseudomallei._

It is predominately a disease of tropical climates, especially in Southeast
Asia and northern Australia where it is widespread. The bacteria causing
melioidosis are found in contaminated water and soil. It is spread to humans
and animals through direct contact with the contaminated source.
