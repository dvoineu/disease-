# West Nile virus

Minus

Related Pages

![Mosquito on white background](/westnile/images/GettyImages-157380357.jpg)

West Nile virus (WNV) is the leading cause of mosquito-borne disease in the
continental United States.  It is most commonly spread to people by the bite
of an infected mosquito. Cases of WNV occur during mosquito season, which
starts in the summer and continues through fall. There are no vaccines to
prevent or medications to treat WNV in people. Fortunately, most people
infected with WNV do not feel sick. About 1 in 5 people who are infected
develop a fever and other symptoms. About 1 out of 150 infected people develop
a serious, sometimes fatal, illness. You can reduce your risk of WNV by using
insect repellent and wearing long-sleeved shirts and long pants to prevent
mosquito bites.
