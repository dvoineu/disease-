# Pertussis (Whooping Cough)

Minus

Related Pages

![Child touching baby's head](/pertussis/images/header_vp4.jpg)

Pertussis, also known as **whooping cough** , is a highly contagious
respiratory disease. It is caused by the bacterium _Bordetella pertussis_.

Pertussis is known for uncontrollable, violent coughing which often makes it
hard to breathe. After cough fits, someone with pertussis often needs to take
deep breaths, which result in a "whooping" sound. Pertussis can affect people
of all ages, but can be very serious, even deadly, for babies less than a year
old.

The best way to protect against pertussis is by getting vaccinated. English |
