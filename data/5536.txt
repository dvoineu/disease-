# Carbapenem-resistant Enterobacteriaceae (CRE)

Minus

Related Pages

Enterobacteriaceae are a large family of different types of germs (bacteria)
that commonly cause infections in healthcare settings. Examples of germs in
the Enterobacteriaceae family include _Escherichia coli_ ( _E. coli_ ) and
_Klebsiella pneumoniae_.

Antibiotic resistance occurs when the germs no longer respond to the
antibiotics designed to kill them. Enterobacteriaceae bacteria are constantly
finding new ways to avoid the effects of the antibiotics used to treat the
infections they cause.

When Enterobacteriaceae develop resistance to the group of antibiotics called
carbapenems, the germs are called carbapenem-resistant Enterobacteriaceae
(CRE).

CRE are difficult to treat because they do not respond to commonly used
antibiotics. Occasionally CRE are resistant to all available antibiotics. CRE
are a threat to public health.
