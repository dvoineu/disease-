# Molluscum Contagiosum

Minus

Related Pages

Molluscum contagiosum is an infection caused by a poxvirus (molluscum
contagiosum virus). The result of the infection is usually a benign, mild skin
disease characterized by lesions (growths) that may appear anywhere on the
body. Within 6-12 months, Molluscum contagiosum typically resolves without
scarring but may take as long as 4 years.

The lesions, known as Mollusca, are small, raised, and usually white, pink, or
flesh-colored with a dimple or pit in the center. They often have a pearly
appearance. They're usually smooth and firm. In most people, the lesions range
from about the size of a pinhead to as large as a pencil eraser (2 to 5
millimeters in diameter). They may become itchy, sore, red, and/or swollen.

Mollusca may occur anywhere on the body including the face, neck, arms, legs,
abdomen, and genital area, alone or in groups. The lesions are rarely found on
the palms of the hands or the soles of the feet.
