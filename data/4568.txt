# Educational Materials

Minus

Related Pages

CDC offers many free resources to protect you and your loved ones from food
poisoning. Download free infographics and posters about why food safety is
important and share that information with others. Engage with us on social
media, such as Facebook and Twitter, and share messages about groups at
highest risk for food poisoning, how to safely handle and prepare meat, fish
and poultry, and tips for handling fruits and vegetables safely. Syndicate
free CDC web content directly to your website without having to update or
maintain it.

Patient Stories

![Learn about real patient stories](/foodsafety/images/comms/patient-
stories-273px.jpg)

These people got sick with infections that came from food or animal contact.
Watch or read their stories to learn how they got sick and what they're doing
now to stay safe.

Social Media

![social-media-thumb-273px](/foodsafety/images/comms/social-media-
thumb-273px.png)

Download and share free graphics and social media messages.

Features

![family sitting around a table](/foodsafety/images/comms/features-
thumb-273px.png)

These CDC feature stories give you information you need to protect yourself
and your families from illnesses caused by germs in food and water.

Videos

![image of food poisoning symptoms video](/foodsafety/images/comms/video-
thumb-273px.png)

View a variety of food safety videos to learn which groups of people are at
higher risk of food poisoning, what symptoms should send you to a doctor, and
the dangers of raw milk.

Infographics

![Protect Yourself WHen Eating Out
