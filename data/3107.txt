# Resources for Emergency Health Professionals

Minus

Related Pages

CDC offers a variety of resources for clinicians, communicators,
laboratorians, emergency planners and responders, and disaster relief
volunteers to prepare them to respond to a public health emergency.

Clinicians (COCA)

![A child receiving an injection from a
clinician.](/images/photobox_clinicians.jpg)

Clinicians can stay up-to-date on the latest CDC guidance on health threats,
find training opportunities, and receive direct email support through
Clinician Outreach and Communication Activity (COCA).

More

Communicators (CERC)

![The logo for the Crisis and Emergency Risk Communication
program](/images/photobox_communicators.jpg)

Communicators can use Crisis and Emergency Risk Communication (CERC)
trainings, tools, and resources to communicate effectively during emergencies.

More

Laboratorians

![A laboratorian looking at slide under a
microscope](/images/photobox_laboratorians.jpg)

Laboratorians can find information on specimen collection and shipping,
testing unknown substances, and the Laboratory Response Network.

More

Emergency Planners and Responders

![A group of emergency responders meeting in a
huddle](/images/photobox_emergency.jpg)

Emergency health professionals can use these trainings, tools, and resources
to effectively prepare for and respond to an emergency.

More

Disaster Relief Volunteers

![A volunteer tending to the victim of an
emergency](/images/photobox_volunteers.jpg)

Volunteers must protect their own health and safety while providing disaster
assistance and relief.

More

Health Alert Network (HAN)

![A heart monitoring machine with the word “ALERT” on
it](/images/photobox_han.jpg)

Public Information Officers and CDC partners can find information about urgent
public health incidents through the Health Alert Network (HAN).

More
