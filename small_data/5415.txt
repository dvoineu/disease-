# Harmful Algal Bloom-Associated Illnesses

Minus

Related Pages

![Harmful Algal Blooms \(HABs\)](/habs/images/habs-homepage-image.jpg)

Harmful algal blooms (HABs) are the rapid growth of algae that can cause harm
to animals, people, or the local ecology. A HAB can look like foam, scum, or
mats on the surface of water and can be different colors. HABs can produce
toxins that have caused a variety of illnesses in people and animals. HABs can
occur in warm fresh, marine, or brackish waters with abundant nutrients and
are becoming more frequent with climate change.
