Progress Toward Regional Measles Elimination - Worldwide

Despite progress, global measles control milestones have not been achieved,
reported measles incidence has increased in five of the six World Health
Organization regions since 2016, and estimated global measles deaths have
increased since 2017. Large measles outbreaks in countries with weak
immunization systems accounted for most reported measles cases. Outbreaks
should serve as opportunities to investigate underlying causes for under-
vaccination and to design and implement specific routine immunization
strengthening activities to prevent future outbreaks.

## Global Health Topics

![Malaria](/globalhealth/newsroom/images/icons/protection.png)

##### GLOBAL HEALTH SECURITY

In today's interconnected world, a disease threat anywhere can become a health
threat to America.

![Malaria](/globalhealth/newsroom/images/icons/mosqito.png)

##### MALARIA

One of the most severe public health problems globally, 2015 saw 212 million
malaria infections worldwide.

![TB](/globalhealth/newsroom/images/icons/lungs.png)

##### TUBERCULOSIS

Despite being preventable and treatable, TB is the leading infectious disease
killer in the world.

![MEASELS](/globalhealth/newsroom/images/icons/dropper.png)

##### MEASLES

Impacting 36 out of every million persons worldwide, measles is a highly
infectious and potentially fatal disease.

![hiv](/globalhealth/newsroom/images/icons/hiv.png)

##### HIV

Thirty-five years after the first cases were reported, HIV is still a leading
cause of death and a health threat to millions.

![TB](/globalhealth/newsroom/images/icons/vaccine.png)

##### POLIO

Polio spreads from person to person invading the brain and spinal cord,
potentially causing paralysis.

![ntdS](/globalhealth/newsroom/images/icons/disease.png)

##### NEGLECTED TROPICAL DISEASES

More than a billion people globally are impacted by these bacterial and
parasitic infections.

![ntdS](/globalhealth/newsroom/images/icons/dropper.png)

##### YELLOW FEVER

About 200,000 vaccine-preventable cases of yellow fever occur every year, 90%
of them in Africa.

![lab sciences](/globalhealth/newsroom/images/icons/microscope.png)

##### LAB SCIENCES

Global laboratory expertise protects Americans and U.S. interests from
emerging health threats at home and overseas.
