# Yellow Fever

Minus

Related Pages

![Aedes mosquito](/yellowfever/images/mosquito-flex.jpg)

The yellow fever virus is found in tropical and subtropical areas of Africa
and South America. The virus is spread to people by the bite of an infected
mosquito. Yellow fever is a very rare cause of illness in U.S. travelers.
Illness ranges from a fever with aches and pains to severe liver disease with
bleeding and yellowing skin (jaundice). Yellow fever infection is diagnosed
based on laboratory testing, a person's symptoms, and travel history. There is
no medicine to treat or cure infection. To prevent getting sick from yellow
fever, use insect repellent, wear long-sleeved shirts and long pants, and get
vaccinated.
