Related Pages

These pages provide an overview of cancer topics that are appropriate for the
season, or support a health awareness day or month.

![](/cancer/dcpc/resources/features/breastcancerawareness/images/women_250.jpg)

Breast Cancer Awareness

Getting mammograms regularly can lower the risk of dying from breast cancer.

![](/cancer/dcpc/resources/features/breastcanceryoungwomen/images/young_woman_250.jpg)

Breast Cancer in Young Women

Learn who is at a higher risk of getting breast cancer at a younger age.

![](/cancer/dcpc/resources/features/cancerandmen/images/cancer_men_250.jpg)

Cancer and Men

Men can lower their risk for several common kinds of cancer.

![](/cancer/dcpc/resources/features/womenandcancer/images/cancer-
women-250.jpg)

Cancer and Women

Learn your family history of cancer and what you can do to lower your cancer
risk.

![](/cancer/dcpc/resources/features/cancerandchildren/images/cancer-
prevention-childhood-250.jpg)

Cancer Prevention Starts in Childhood

You can lower your children's risk of getting cancer later in life.

![](/cancer/flu/images/CancerFlu_250px.jpg)

Cancer, the Flu, and You

Cancer patients and survivors are at higher risk of developing serious flu
complications.

![](/cancer/dcpc/resources/features/cervicalcancer/images/CervicalCancer_250px.jpg)

Cervical Cancer Awareness

You can help prevent cervical cancer by getting screened regularly, starting
at age 21.

![](/cancer/colorectal/basic_info/images/robert-250.jpg)

Colorectal Cancer Awareness

If you're 50 to 75 years old, get screened for colorectal cancer regularly.

![](/cancer/dcpc/resources/features/gynecologiccancers/images/gynecologiccancers_250px.jpg)

Gynecologic Cancer Awareness

Learn how to lower your risk for cervical, ovarian, uterine, vaginal, and
vulvar cancers.

![](/cancer/dcpc/resources/features/cancersurvivorship/images/survivor-
flowers-250.jpg)

How to Stay Healthy After Cancer Treatment Ends

You can lower your chance of getting cancer again.

![](/cancer/dcpc/resources/features/lungcancer/images/lung-couple-250.jpg)

Lung Cancer Awareness

To lower your lung cancer risk, don't smoke, avoid secondhand smoke, and get
your home tested for radon.

![](/cancer/dcpc/resources/features/preventinfections/images/PreventInfections_250px.jpg)

Preventing Infections in Cancer Patients

Call your doctor right away if you get a fever or feel sick during your
chemotherapy treatment.

![](/cancer/dcpc/resources/features/prostatecancer/images/ProstateCancer-
man-250.jpg)

Prostate Cancer Awareness

Learn about prostate cancer and talk to your doctor before you decide to get
tested or treated for prostate cancer.

![](/cancer/dcpc/resources/features/skincancer/images/beach-bag-250.jpg)

Skin Cancer Awareness

Make sun safety an everyday habit so you can lower your chance of getting skin
cancer.

![](/cancer/dcpc/resources/features/worldcancerday/images/world-cancer-
day-250.jpg)

World Cancer Day

You can help create a future without cancer, no matter where you live.
