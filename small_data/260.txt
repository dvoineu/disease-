# Diseases & the Vaccines that Prevent Them

Minus

Related Pages

<< Back to Vaccines Home

Because of vaccines, some diseases (like polio and diphtheria) are becoming
rare in the U.S. Vaccination can prevent certain deadly diseases in infants,
children, teens, adults and travelers of all ages.

See below for recommended and non-routine vaccines by disease in the U.S.,
vaccines recommended by age and travel destination, and detailed vaccination
and disease information.

Recommended U.S. immunizations and non-routine immunizations for people in

![group of people of various ages](/vaccines/vpd/images/home-all-ages.jpg)

These vaccines are recommended for infants, children, teenagers and adults of
certain ages.

More

Vaccines For Travelers

![plane in flight above the clouds](/vaccines/vpd/images/home-travel.jpg)

Travelers may need these vaccines because there is a risk of certain diseases
in countries they are visiting.

More

Who Should NOT Get Vaccinated With These Vaccines?

Some people should not get certain vaccinations or should wait before
receiving a vaccine. Find out who should avoid which vaccines.

More

What Are the Possible Side Effects from Vaccines?

Any vaccine can cause side effects, which are usually minor. These are the
