# Get Involved

Minus

Related Pages

![Get Involved](/cpr/prepareyourhealth/00-images/GetInvolved-Header.jpg)

You have the ability to improve the health resilience of family, friends,
neighbors, and your community at large through your actions. Seek out and help
raise awareness of resources and training and volunteer opportunities in your
community that can help individuals protect their health until help arrives.
Simple things, such as posting a #SafeAndWellSelfie to social media and
sharing a link to the Ready Wrigley activity books with your child's teacher,
can help create community resilience.

The Basics

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        * Join a local Medical Reserve Corps (MRC)external icon Unit or Community Emergency Response Team (CERT).
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        * Contact your state and local public health departments to help with a Community Assessment for Public Health Emergency Response (CASPER) or participate in a readiness exercises.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        * Give blood.

Quick Tips

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        * Talk to family and friends about how to prepare for an emergency. Research by FEMA shows that talking about preparedness increases the likelihood of others taking steps to get prepared.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        * Volunteer with a local faith-based or non-government organization involved in disaster response.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        * Build a personal support network of people who can provide transportation or evacuation assistance if you are an older adult, have a disability, or live alone.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        * Train with the National Weather Service (NWS) to become a NWS SKYWARN Storm Spotterexternal icon.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        * Join your workplace emergency team or start a CERT programexternal icon at work. CERT has trained some 600,000 volunteers nationwide.
