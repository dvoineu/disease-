### New report: U.S. Cryptosporidiosis Outbreaks 2009 - 2017

_Cryptosporidium_ is a microscopic parasite that causes the diarrheal disease
cryptosporidiosis. Both the parasite and the disease are commonly known as
"Crypto."

There are many species of _Cryptosporidium_ that infect animals, some of which
also infect humans. The parasite is protected by an outer shell that allows it
to survive outside the body for long periods of time and makes it very
tolerant to chlorine disinfection.

While this parasite can be spread in several different ways, water (drinking
water and recreational water) is the most common way to spread the parasite.
_Cryptosporidium_ is a leading cause of waterborne disease among humans in the
United States.
