RMSF

One of the deadliest tickborne diseases in the Americas

Learn more

**Rocky Mountain spotted fever** (RMSF) is a bacterial disease spread through
the bite of an infected tick. Most people who get sick with RMSF will have a
fever, headache, and rash. RMSF can be deadly if not treated early with the
right antibiotic.

Avoid ticks

There is no vaccine for RMSF. The first step for preventing this devastating
disease is to prevent tick bites.

![Clip art image of a tick being pulled from skin by a pair of
tweezers.](/rmsf/images/home/tick-tweezers.jpg)

Remove ticks

If you find a tick attached to your skin, there's no need to panic--the key is
to remove the tick as soon as possible.

![Map of the U.S. showing where American dog ticks can be found. They can be
located in the eastern half of the U.S. and
California.](/rmsf/images/home/AmericanDogTick-Map.jpg)

Where ticks live

Not all ticks bite and transmit disease to people. Find out what which ticks
are spreading disease in your area.
